# Boring Business AI - Brand Guidelines

## Brand Overview

**Brand Name:** boring business AI
**Tagline:** real ai. real results. for real business.
**Brand Voice:** Practical, approachable, results-driven, unpretentious

## Brand Story

Boring Business AI demystifies artificial intelligence for real businesses. No hype, no buzzwords—just practical AI solutions that deliver measurable results. The brand personifies this through the "AI OPS" character: a skilled technician who makes AI work like any other essential business tool.

## Typography

### Primary Font: Bold Sans-Serif

**Brand Title Usage:**
- Font style: Heavy/Black weight sans-serif
- Always lowercase: "boring business AI"
- Letter spacing: Normal to slightly tight
- Color: Orange (#FF8C00)
- Never use all caps, never use title case

**Tagline Typography:**
- Font style: Regular weight sans-serif, italic
- Always lowercase: "real ai. real results. for real business."
- Color: White on dark backgrounds
- Placement: Below main title

**Recommended Font Families:**
- Montserrat (Black/Heavy for title, Regular for body)
- Inter (Bold for title, Regular for body)
- Poppins (Bold for title, Regular for body)
- Work Sans (Bold for title, Regular for body)

---

## Background Patterns

### Blueprint Pattern

**Style:** Technical schematic/blueprint aesthetic
- Grid lines and technical diagrams
- Circuit board style connections
- Subtle, non-distracting
- Color: Lighter blue lines on darker blue background
- Opacity: 20-30% to avoid overwhelming content

**Usage:**
- Headers and hero sections
- Course covers and banners
- Social media backgrounds
- Email headers

**When NOT to use:**
- Over complex imagery
- When text readability is compromised
- For minimalist designs requiring clean backgrounds

## Logo Usage Guidelines

### Clear Space

Maintain clear space around logo equal to the height of the "AI OPS" cap in the logo character.

### Minimum Sizes

- Digital: 120px width minimum
- Print: 1 inch width minimum
- Profile pictures: 400x400px minimum

### Don'ts

❌ Don't stretch or distort the logo
❌ Don't change the brand colors
❌ Don't rotate the logo
❌ Don't add effects (shadows, glows, etc.)
❌ Don't place logo on busy backgrounds without proper contrast
❌ Don't separate the character from the title in primary uses
❌ Don't use title case or all caps for brand name

### Correct Usage

✅ Use provided logo files as-is
✅ Maintain aspect ratio when resizing
✅ Use appropriate variant for context (with/without tagline)
✅ Ensure sufficient contrast with background
✅ Use icon-only version for small applications (favicon, app icon)

## Brand Voice & Messaging

### Tone

- **Straightforward:** No fluff, no hype
- **Practical:** Focus on real-world applications
- **Approachable:** Demystify AI, make it accessible
- **Confident:** We know what works
- **Unpretentious:** "Boring" is a badge of honor

### Key Messages

1. AI doesn't have to be complicated
2. Real results matter more than flashy tech
3. Business fundamentals + AI = competitive advantage
4. Practical implementation over theoretical possibilities
5. Measurable ROI, not vague promises

### Words We Use

- Practical, straightforward, results-driven
- Implementation, execution, operations
- Real business, real world, real results
- Measurable, proven, tested
- Efficient, effective, reliable

### Words We Avoid

- Revolutionary, game-changing, disruptive (unless truly applicable)
- Synergy, leverage, paradigm shift
- Magic, miracle, incredible (over-hype)
- Complex jargon without explanation
- Vague promises without specifics

## Design Principles

### 1. Clarity Over Cleverness

Information should be immediately clear. Don't sacrifice readability for visual flair.

### 2. Consistency Builds Trust

Use consistent colors, fonts, and layouts across all materials.

### 3. Orange for Action

Use orange for CTAs, emphasis, and anything requiring user attention.

### 4. Blue for Foundation

Blue backgrounds create professional, trustworthy foundation.

### 5. Space Matters

Don't overcrowd. Give elements room to breathe.

### 6. Technical but Friendly

Blueprint patterns add technical credibility without being cold or intimidating.

## Common Use Cases

- Orange text for emphasis
- Logo presence (corner or watermark)
- High contrast for mobile viewing

- Full logo centered
- Blue background
- Presentation title in white below logo
- Date/presenter info in smaller text
